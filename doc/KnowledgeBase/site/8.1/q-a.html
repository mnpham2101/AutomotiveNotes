8.1 20-Day Training Plan

Q&A

  • Q: What if 20 days isn't enough to feel ready? A: Repeat Phase 3 (hands-on) and Phase 5 (leadership drills) — they're the highest-leverage, most perishable skills; conceptual recall (Phases 1–2/4) degrades slower and needs less repetition.
  • Q: Should this be done in order, or can phases be reordered? A: Order matters for Phases 1→3 (each builds on the prior), but Phase 4 topics (6.1, 7.1) can be moved earlier if a real project's context (e.g. an upcoming customer meeting) makes them urgent.
  • Q: Is Phase 6 mandatory, or only for someone aiming deeper than the core role? A: It's the part of the plan designed to scale to a team rather than one trainee — run Days 1–20 once as shared onboarding, then run Phase 6 as a rotating weekly knowledge-sharing slot (one topic, one presenter, one week) so the research burden is distributed instead of falling on one person re-reading the full 193-page spec alone.
  • Q: Why is the Workflow Example saved for last (Day 28) instead of being folded into Phase 3's hands-on work? A: By Day 28 the trainee already has the 2.1 call chain, the 4.1 hands-on build, and the 6.1 methodology fresh — that's what makes it possible to recognize the real meta-model classes in the Workflow Example's ARXML on sight rather than learning AUTOSAR meta-model vocabulary cold.
